Calculate contract rate

To work out a rough contracting rate based on your existing permanent salary and a few other factors.

#{form @Application.calculate()}
Salary

How much would your earn as an employee.

Cost

How much do you actually cost your employer. (Benefits, insurance, office space, admin, company tax, training, etc) Some say another 30%, others 50%, 2x or even 3x your salary. Or rather how much extra will contracting cost you. (Commuting, hardware, insurance, no holidays, admin, sales, training cost, etc) If you want to cut things to the bone, pick a number closer to 1.00. I use 1.50.

Hours per year

How many hours will you be available to work per year. 365 days per year and 5 working days per week is 260 days. However you may work 8, 7.5 or 7 hours per day. You may want holidays or you may be sick. You may want to take time off for training and conferences. The actual average varies depending on country. The US is actually closer to 2000 hours, the OECD average is around 1750, while in Norway it is just under 1500. With 8 bank holidays, 25 normal holidays, 5 days off sick, and 7 days of training, I use 1600 hours.

Hours per day

Not including lunch

Utilisation

How long a gap will you have between contracts. It is difficult to finish one contract on the Friday and always having another starting the following Monday. Some bench time must be expected, 2 weeks here, a week there or worse. I use 90% utilisation.

Tax difference

As a contractor you might pay less tax personally compared to as a permanenet employee. However in the UK, IR35 means umbrella contractors pay a little more than before. (1.00 means no difference, 1.10 means 10% more)
