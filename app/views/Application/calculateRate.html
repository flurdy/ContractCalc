#{form @Application.calculateRate()}
Salary

How much would your earn as an employee per year before tax (currency irrelevant).

Hours per year

How many hours will you be available to work per year(invoice). 365 days per year and 5 working days per week is 260 days. However you may work 8, 7.5 or 7 hours per day. You may want holidays or you may be sick. You may want to take time off for training and conferences. The actual average varies depending on country. The US is actually closer to 2000 hours, the OECD average is around 1750, while in Norway it is just under 1500. With 8 bank holidays, 25 normal holidays, 5 days off sick, and 7 days of training, I use 1600 hours.

Hours per day

Not including lunch. I use 7.5 per day.

Additional cost

1.00 is no additional cost. 1.1 means 10% additional costs. I use 1.50.

If you are looking for how much you can earn in relation to your current salary, then enter your estimated additional cost to the employer.

The employee's salary is not the employers only cost per employee. The company also have to pay for your benefits, insurance, tax, office space, utilities, admin staff, training, hardware, licenses, liabilities, etc. This can be as low as additional 30% cost per employee, however in some countries and companies this can be 2x or 3x the salary.

However if you are looking for what you need to charge for equivalent salary, then you need to enter your estimated additional costs as a contractor.

As a self employed contractor you have to pay for your pension, insurance, accountants, PCs, mobiles, tools, training, conferences, commuting cost, non-invoiceable time for admin & sales, etc. If you want to cut things to the bone, pick a number closer to 1.00.

Utilisation

How long a gap will you have between contracts. It is difficult to finish one contract on the Friday and always having another starting the following Monday. Some bench time must be expected, 2 weeks here, a week there or worse. I use 90% utilisation.

Tax difference

As a contractor you might pay less tax personally compared to as a permanent employee. However in the UK, IR35 means umbrella contractors pay a little more than before. (1.00 means no difference, 1.10 means 10% more)
